The Core Concept

Price frequently sweeps beyond session highs and lows before reversing. This is a structural pattern that repeats across instruments — particularly in futures markets — driven by stop runs, liquidity grabs, and institutional order flow. Quantum STS is built around this behaviour.

Each session establishes a high and a low. When the session closes, the indicator marks entry levels at configurable offsets beyond those extremes — short above the high, long below the low. If price sweeps to those levels in the following session and reverses, that's the trade.

The two tools in this system serve different roles. Quantum STS is the live indicator — it draws sessions, marks levels, shows R:R boxes, and fires alerts in real time. Q-STS Backtester runs the same logic on historical data so you can measure how your offset, stop, and TP settings have performed before committing real capital to them.

Default settings are sized for MNQ1!

Both tools ship with settings configured for Micro E-mini Nasdaq futures. If you're trading a different instrument, review your stop and TP point values before using either tool live. What's a reasonable stop on MNQ can be either too tight or wildly oversized on ES, NQ, or other contracts.

The Five Sessions

Both tools use the same five sessions. Each has its own timezone, start and end time, color, and independent entry and R:R configuration.

Asia

Default: 09:00–15:00 Tokyo. Sets levels used during the London session.

London

Default: 08:00–13:00 London. Sets levels used during NY Pre-Open.

NY Pre-Open

Default: 08:00–09:30 New York. Sets levels used during NY AM.

NY AM

Default: 09:30–11:30 New York. Sets levels used during NY PM.

NY PM

Default: 13:00–16:00 New York. Sets levels used for the next Asia session.

Each session can be shown or hidden independently. Session names, colors, and times are all configurable. The cascade order is fixed — each session's extremes feed directly into the next session's entry levels.

How Entry Levels Are Generated

At the close of each session, the indicator takes that session's confirmed high and low and calculates two entry prices:

  • Short level — session high + short offset (in points)
  • Long level — session low − long offset (in points)

An offset of 0 means the entry is placed exactly at the session extreme. Increasing the offset means price has to travel further beyond the level before the entry triggers. This filters out minor sweeps and requires a more decisive move.

Each direction can be enabled or disabled independently. If you only want to trade longs from a particular session, turn off short entries for that group. The levels that are disabled simply won't be drawn or traded.

Offset tuning

Start with 0. After reviewing results in the backtester, increase offset only if you're seeing a high rate of entries that immediately reverse without reaching your TP. Adding offset reduces signal frequency but improves entry quality on instruments with noisy sweeps.

Quantum STS — Indicator Features

Quantum STS is the live, chart-side component. It handles everything you need during a session: session boxes, entry level lines, R:R visualization, key levels, VWAP, and alerts.

Session Boxes

Each active session is drawn as a colored box that expands in real time as the high and low update. When the session closes, the box locks. A history setting controls how many past boxes remain visible — the default keeps the last five sessions per type. If the chart gets cluttered, reduce this number.

Box borders are off by default. Enable them in Box Styling if you want a harder edge on each session range. Label size is adjustable between tiny, small, and normal.

Order Lines

When a session ends, the indicator draws dotted lines at the calculated entry levels for the next session. Each line is labeled — L for long, S for short — with color-coded dots matching direction. Line width, label size, and the number of bars the line extends are all configurable under Order Lines settings.

R:R Boxes

Each entry level can display an R:R box showing the stop and take profit zones visually on the chart. The box is drawn at the moment the level is created — before price reaches it — so you can assess the setup before it triggers.

The box shows two zones: a green fill between the entry and TP, and a red fill between the entry and stop. Stop and TP sizes are pulled from the per-session R:R settings — the same values you configure in the backtester. This means what you see on the chart matches exactly what the backtester is simulating.

R:R boxes can be toggled on or off per direction per session. If you only want to see the short setup visualized for London, disable the long R:R box for that session group without affecting the level line itself.

Key Static Levels

Under Key Levels, you can overlay structural price references that update automatically:

  • Prev Day H/L — Previous day's high and low, drawn as solid lines. Default on.
  • Prev Week H/L — Previous week's high and low.
  • Prev Month H/L — Previous month's high and low.
  • Day Open / Week Open / Month Open — Opening prices for each period, drawn as dashed lines.
  • Round Numbers — Automatically draws dotted lines at configurable point intervals (default every 50 points). Useful for identifying psychological levels.

Each level type has its own color. Lines are persistent and extend to the right edge of the chart. They update on each new bar when TradingView recalculates.

Session H/L Extensions

When enabled per session, dotted lines extend rightward from the session high and low after it closes. These persist until the same session starts again the following day. The purpose is to keep the previous session's range visible during the next session — useful for reading where price is relative to the range it's already swept.

Extensions are on by default for all five sessions. Toggle them off per session in that session's group settings if you find them cluttering the chart.

VWAP

An optional VWAP plot with three anchor options:

  • Session — Resets at the start of each session. Gives you a VWAP relative to the current session's traded volume.
  • Week — Resets each Monday. Standard weekly VWAP.
  • Month — Resets on the first bar of each calendar month.

VWAP is off by default. Color and line thickness are configurable. If you use VWAP as a bias filter — only taking longs above, shorts below — the Session anchor is the most useful given the structure of this system.

Alerts

Quantum STS includes two layers of alerts: named conditions that appear in TradingView's alert dropdown, and dynamic alerts that fire with price and session context embedded in the message.

Session alerts fire when:

  • Any session starts
  • Any session ends and new entry levels are drawn (includes the session high and low in the message)

Level touch alerts fire when:

  • Price crosses the long entry level or the short entry level for the active session
  • Each level fires once per bar close — it won't re-trigger on the same level in the same session

VWAP alerts fire when:

  • Price crosses above or below VWAP

To set up an alert, right-click the indicator on your chart, select "Add alert on Quantum STS", and choose the condition you want. For webhook automation, paste your webhook URL in the Notifications tab. The dynamic messages include the ticker symbol and relevant prices, so a single alert setup works across multiple instruments if you add the indicator to different charts.

Level touch alert behaviour

Alert state resets when a new session starts and levels are recalculated. If price crosses a level, pulls back, and crosses it again within the same session cycle, the second cross will not fire another alert. This is intentional — it prevents duplicate notifications on choppy price action around a level.

Q-STS Backtester — How It Works

The backtester runs the same session cascade logic as the indicator on historical bars. When a session closes, it places limit orders at the calculated entry levels for the next session. If price reaches those levels, a position is simulated. Stop loss and take profit are applied, and the result is logged through TradingView's built-in strategy tester.

This gives you a direct performance record for any combination of offset, stop, and TP settings on your chosen instrument and timeframe — before you use those settings on a live or funded account.

⚠️ One position per direction per session

The backtester is limited to one long and one short per session cycle. All open positions are force-closed at the end of each session cycle regardless of profit or loss. This is intentional — it enforces clean, measurable results and prevents overlapping trades from distorting the equity curve.

Entry and Exit Logic

When a session ends, the backtester cancels any unfilled orders from the previous cycle, closes any open position, resets the trade flags, and places two new limit orders at the calculated levels for the upcoming session.

When price fills a limit order, the position is opened. The direction flag is set and the other order is cancelled — so only one trade per direction runs at a time. Exit orders are placed immediately using the stop and TP values for that session group. If your TP or stop is hit intrabar, the fill is registered at that level.

Per-Session Stop and TP

Stop loss and take profit are configured separately for longs and shorts within each session group. That means you can set tighter parameters for Asia — which typically has a narrower range — and wider parameters for NY AM, which can move significantly more in the same timeframe.

All values are in points. The settings work correctly across different futures contracts as long as you input values appropriate for the instrument you're testing.

Per-session R:R settings in the backtester:

  • Short Stop (pts) / Short TP (pts)
  • Long Stop (pts) / Long TP (pts)

These are independent for each of the five session groups. You're not locked into a single R:R profile for all sessions — you can and should tune them separately based on each session's historical behaviour on your instrument.

Using Them Together

The correct sequence is: configure and test in the backtester, then apply validated settings to the live indicator. They share the same session logic and the same input structure, so settings transfer directly.

Add Q-STS Backtester to the same chart and timeframe you intend to trade

Run it on the same timeframe you intend to trade.

Configure session times and offsets

Start with offsets at 0 and the default stop/TP values. Check the Strategy Tester results — net profit, win rate, and drawdown.

Tune per-session R:R settings

Open each session group and adjust stop and TP to match that session's typical range on your instrument. Compare results.

Transfer settings to Quantum STS

Copy your session times, offsets, and per-session stop/TP values into the live indicator. The inputs are structured identically. What you validated in the backtester is exactly what the indicator will visualize and alert on.

Use the indicator for live execution

Monitor R:R boxes to preview setups before they trigger. Set alerts to fire once per bar when price reaches an entry level. Track live sessions with VWAP and key levels for additional context.

Risk Disclaimer

⚠️ Important: Read This Carefully

This is not financial advice. Trading carries real risk and you can lose money. If you decide to use these tools, that is your choice and your responsibility.

Backtests do not guarantee future performance. Markets change — what worked over the past six months may not work over the next six. Use the backtester to understand the behaviour of your settings, not to predict future results.

The default settings are a starting point, not a finished system. Expect to spend time on the backtester before treating any configuration as ready for live trading. If you are using a funded or prop firm account, understand the drawdown limits and make sure your position sizing and stop distances are compatible with those limits before running anything live.
